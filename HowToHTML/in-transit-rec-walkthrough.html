In Transit Reconciliation Walkthrough

This guide walks you through reconciling the In Transit clearing account in RapidReconciler from start to finish. By the end of the process you will be able to confirm the In Transit GL balance ties to open transfer order activity, identify and resolve every source of variance, post the correct journal entries in JD Edwards, and produce a complete audit trail for period-end close.

Quick Reference — The Process at a Glance

The reconciliation is a seven-step process. Each step builds on the previous one — confirm system health first, triage open orders, then quantify and resolve each source of variance.

#StepGoal
1 Log in & verify system health Both status lights green
2 Review and triage open orders Exclude closed-but-unbalanced pairs
3 Read the Reconciliation page Identify the Out of Balance amount
4 Resolve each variance source Address every non-zero variance line
5 Investigate transaction-level variances Understand root cause of each item
6 Post journal entries and close Clear the remaining variance
7 Run integrity reports & save audit Confirm clean configuration; document the close
Key principle

RapidReconciler does not correct In Transit issues. All corrections happen in JD Edwards or through manual journal entries. RapidReconciler's job is to tell you exactly which orders are unbalanced, by how much, and why — so you can take the right action.

What Is Being Reconciled

When goods are shipped from Branch A to Branch B, their value sits in a clearing account (typically via DMAAI 4220 or 4245) until they are received at the destination. If the shipment and receipt do not match in both quantity and amount, a balance remains in the clearing account that must be explained and cleared.

Order Pairs Explained

The order pair is the core concept behind the Orders page. The Orders page matches each shipment to its corresponding receipt and presents them as a single row — that row is the order pair. Understanding how this matching works is essential before starting the seven-step process, because exclusions, variances, and the In Transit balance itself all flow from it.

Scope

Order pairs are specific to the Orders page. The Reconciliation, Transactions, and As-Of pages each present a different view of the data and are not organized by order pair.

What Is an Order Pair?

A transfer of goods between two branches in JD Edwards is recorded as two related orders:

Shipping Side
ST Order

Transfer sales order created at the source branch when goods are shipped out.

Receiving Side
OT Order

Transfer purchase order created at the destination branch when goods are received in.

An order pair is the single, summarized view of one ST + one OT working together at the item number level. The same physical transfer of an item appears as one row in the In Transit module — not two — because both sides of the transfer are matched into a single record.

How the Pairing Works

The Orders page matches each shipment to its corresponding receipt using related order data stored on the sales order. When a transfer order is created in JD Edwards, the F4211 record carries a reference back to the originating purchase order, and RapidReconciler uses this link to bring the two sides together.

The match key

RapidReconciler pairs the ST sales order to the OT purchase order using the related order fields on F4211. The pair is then summarized at the item number level — one ST + one OT + one item = one row.

What an Order Pair Tells You

Once paired, RapidReconciler calculates the in-transit position for each order pair by subtracting receipts from shipments:

FieldCalculationWhat It Means
TranQtyShip Qty − Rec QtyQuantity still in transit between the two branches
TranAmtShip Amt − Rec AmtDollar value still sitting in the In Transit clearing account

When TranQty and TranAmt are both zero, the pair is fully cleared — quantity shipped equals quantity received, and the dollar value has cleared the In Transit account. Any non-zero value means there is open transfer activity that contributes to the In Transit balance.

Why This Matters for Reconciliation

  • The In Transit balance is the sum of every open pair's TranAmt. The total at the bottom of the Orders page rolls up directly into the In Transit Balance shown on the Reconciliation page.
  • Exclusions happen at the pair level. When you exclude an order in Step 2, you're telling RapidReconciler to stop counting that one ST/OT/item combination toward the In Transit balance.
  • Cost variances surface at the pair level. Integrity Report 4 examines each pair to find cases where the ST sales price doesn't equal the OT purchase cost — these will never clear without action.
  • Open pairs are the source of every variance you'll investigate. Whatever you see on the Reconciliation page can be traced back to one or more open pairs on the Orders page — which is why Step 2 (triaging the Orders page) comes before any other corrective action.
Note on terminology

Although this guide refers to ST/OT order types, RapidReconciler detects different transfer types based on a flag in the F4211 table. The pairing logic is the same regardless of the specific order type codes used in your environment.

1 Step One

Log In and Verify System Health

Goal
Confirm the system is ready for reconciliation work.

Procedure

  1. Log in to RapidReconciler at https://rapidreconciler.getgsi.com.
  2. Navigate to the In Transit module. The Reconciliation page opens by default.
  3. Use the period selector (top right) to choose the period being reconciled.
  4. Apply company and account filters as needed (Company → Business Unit → Object → Subsidiary).
  5. Verify the two status indicators:
IndicatorGreen MeansIf Red
In Transit Validation The carry-forward from prior period is accurate Hover for details; resolve the prior period before proceeding
System Status The JD Edwards import completed successfully Hover for details; contact your administrator
Yellow status

If the System Status light is flashing yellow, the import is in progress. Wait for it to finish before continuing.

Stop condition

Do not proceed to Step 2 if either light is red. Fixing the underlying issue first prevents wasted work.

2 Step Two

Review and Triage Open Orders

Goal
Make sure the Orders page lists only what is truly in transit. Everything else must be excluded.

Filter Buttons on the Orders Page

At the top of the Orders grid are three buttons that automatically filter the result set. Each one isolates a specific class of order pair that needs attention.

Receipts without Shipments

If the age of the receipt is large, this is more than likely a timing issue with the start date — the order should probably be excluded. If the date is more recent, contact the shipping branch to investigate a potential missing transaction.

Items in Transit with Closed PO

Orders where the lowest next status on any line is 999 and there is a non-zero quantity in transit. These orders need to be investigated as the quantity shipped does not agree with the quantity received.

Zero Quantity in Transit

Orders where the ship quantity matches the receipt quantity but there is an amount mismatch that must be excluded. Any exclusion amount adds to the Exclusion variance source and will be cleared during the close.

The goal

There should be no data showing for any of the three filters. Each filter that returns rows is a list of orders that need triage before the period can be reconciled cleanly.

Procedure

  1. Navigate to the Orders page.
  2. Review every order pair. For each one, decide its disposition:
SituationAction
Both ST and OT at status 999 with remaining balanceExclude — no further JD Edwards activity is possible
OT not yet received or partially received; more receipts expectedLeave open — legitimately in transit
Partial receipt; remaining quantity unclearInvestigate with the warehouse before excluding
Cost variance only; quantity is zeroExclude and post a JE for the dollar variance
  1. Before excluding, research each order in JD Edwards to confirm no further activity will occur.
  2. Check the ExclVarQty (Excluded Quantity Variance) and ExclVarAmt (Excluded Amount Variance) columns. Any non-zero value means new transactions have hit a previously excluded order — you must unexclude and re-exclude to recalculate.
  3. Note the total Exclusions amount at the bottom of the page. This becomes your offsetting journal entry amount in Step 6.
What is an exclusion?

Excluding an order pair tells RapidReconciler to stop counting it toward the In Transit balance. It does not change anything in JD Edwards — the GL balance still needs an offsetting journal entry.

Order pairs must be investigated in JD Edwards before they are excluded. Although an exclusion can be reversed at any time by unexcluding the order, exclusions made without first confirming the underlying cause can mask real issues — missing transactions, DMAAI configuration problems, or transfer cost variances that need to be corrected at the source.

The Recalc Balance Button

RapidReconciler's data refreshes nightly, but exclusion changes you make on the Orders page do not need to wait for the next refresh cycle. Above the Orders grid (visible in the screenshot at the top of this page) is a Recalc Balance button.

When to use itWhat it does
After excluding or unexcluding one or more order pairs Immediately updates the In Transit Balance and variance calculations on the Reconciliation page so you can see the impact of your triage work without waiting for the nightly refresh.
Workflow

Complete all your exclusion decisions for the period first, then click Recalc Balance once. There is no need to recalc after every individual exclusion — batching them is faster and produces the same result.

Reminder

Recalc Balance only updates the calculations driven by Orders page activity (exclusions and the In Transit Balance). Journal entries posted in JD Edwards still require the next nightly refresh before they appear in RapidReconciler.

4 Step Four

Resolve Each Variance Source

Goal
Walk down the Variance Calculation section and address every non-zero line. The sum of all lines equals the Out of Balance amount.

Variance Lines at a Glance

Variance LineMust Be Zero to Close?Resolution
Carry ForwardInvestigateResolve in prior period or roll into current JE
GL BatchesYesWork with finance to post all open batches
End of DayYesConfirm Sales Update (R42800) ran for transfer orders
TransactionsInvestigateDrill in via Step 5 and prepare offsetting JEs
ExclusionsPrepare JEPost journal entry for the excluded amount
Manual Journal EntriesInformationalConfirm entries are correctly reflected

Carry Forward

The unresolved out-of-balance amount from the prior period.

Resolve by: Returning to the prior period to investigate, or — if the prior period is closed — including the amount in the current period's manual journal entry. Document the reason.

GL Batches

Entries in F0911 where the posted code is not "P" (not yet posted to the GL).

Resolve by: Working with finance to post the outstanding batches. A bell icon flags batches more than 2 days old — prioritize these. If a batch header is missing, run JD Edwards' "Missing Batch Header" report.

Required

GL Batches must be zero before performing closing activities.

End of Day

Transfer sales orders that have been ship confirmed but not yet processed through Sales Update (R42800/P42800).

Resolve by: Confirming the Sales Update version configured for transfer orders is scheduled and completing successfully. The In Transit Sales Update version must have the A/R interface turned off. A bell icon flags orders more than 2 days old.

Required

End of Day must be zero before closing. Until Sales Update runs, the shipment has no batch number or GL date.

Transactions

Documents where the order lines (F4211 sales orders or F4311 purchase orders) do not match the GL (F0911). Common causes: DMAAI mismatches, fiscal period differences, account number discrepancies.

Resolve by: Going to the Transactions page (Step 5) to investigate each item. The corrective action is always an offsetting JE in JD Edwards plus a note in RapidReconciler.

Exclusions

The "leftover" balance from order pairs you excluded in Step 2.

Resolve by: Posting a journal entry in JD Edwards — debit or credit the In Transit account and offset to an appropriate variance or expense account per your organization's policy.

Manual Journal Entries

Manual entries posted directly to the In Transit GL account. Informational only — confirms they are reflected in the variance calculation.

5 Step Five

Investigate Transaction-Level Variances

Goal
Determine the root cause of every transaction variance and prepare corrective journal entries.

Procedure

  1. Navigate to the Transactions page.
  2. Note that transactions differing by less than 1 monetary unit are filtered by default but still counted in totals (administrator can adjust the tolerance).
  3. For each row, click the + icon on the left to open the Transaction Detail report. The report has three sections:
SectionContents
1 — F0911 GLAll F0911 rows for the company, document type, and document number
2 — Order LinesFor sales shipments, the F4211 sales order lines. For PO receipts, the F4311 purchase order lines.
3 — DMAAIsAll DMAAI entries for each GL class code (first row = model table)

Analysis Tips

  • Verify company number, account number, and period ending date match across Sections 1 and 2.
  • If account numbers differ, consult the DMAAI setup in Section 3.
  • Refer to the DMAAI Reference Guide for configuration help.
  1. Determine the root cause: DMAAI mismatch, period mismatch, or account mismatch.
  2. Prepare an offsetting journal entry in JD Edwards for each variance.
  3. Select the row on the Transactions page and click the Edit Note button to document the corrective action and (optionally) mark the row as worked.

The Edit Note Popup

Clicking Edit Note opens a popup that combines two actions in a single dialog:

FieldPurpose
Worked checkboxMarks the transaction as worked. Worked rows can be filtered off the grid using the Worked Transactions dropdown — but totals are not affected.
Note inputFree-text field for documenting the corrective action. Notes appear on the period-end audit report.
Best practice

Include your name and date in every note. Notes appear on the period-end audit report and become the audit trail for how each variance was resolved.

The Worked Transactions Filter

The Worked Transactions dropdown above the grid controls which rows are visible. It has three values:

ValueWhat It Shows
NoOnly transactions that have not yet been marked as worked. This is the working view during reconciliation — it leaves only what still needs attention.
YesOnly transactions that have been marked as worked. Useful for reviewing what's been documented and confirming notes are complete before close.
AllEvery transaction, regardless of worked status. Use this when reconciling totals or auditing.
Important

The Worked Transactions filter is a view filter only. It changes which rows appear on the grid but does not change the variance totals on the Reconciliation page. Marking a transaction worked does not resolve it — the underlying journal entry must still be posted in JD Edwards.

6 Step Six

Post Journal Entries and Close

Goal
Clear the remaining variance and confirm the period reconciles.

Pre-Close Checklist

    Confirm before posting
  • GL Batches = $0
  • End of Day = $0
  • All transaction variances reviewed and notes documented

Calculate the Closing Journal Entry Total

Once GL Batches and End of Day are at zero, the remaining Out of Balance is composed of three resolvable variance lines. The closing journal entry is a single combined entry that totals these three values to bring the Out of Balance to zero:

Closing JE Formula
Carry Forward  +  Transactions  +  Exclusions

The sum of all unresolved variances. Posting this entry should drive Out of Balance to $0.

ComponentWhat It Represents
Carry ForwardThe unresolved out-of-balance amount carried over from the prior period (when the prior period is closed and cannot be returned to).
TransactionsThe net total of all F4211 (sales order) or F4311 (purchase order) vs F0911 mismatches investigated in Step 5.
ExclusionsThe leftover balance from order pairs excluded in Step 2.
Why a single entry

Combining the three lines into one journal entry simplifies the audit trail and ensures the period closes cleanly. The Manual Journal Entries line on the Reconciliation page will reflect the posted total once the next nightly refresh runs, and the Out of Balance should be exactly zero.

Posting the Journal Entry

Use This ToolWhen
Journal Entry buttonOn the Reconciliation page — produces an Excel report of GL and In Transit balances to support account-level entries.
Offset Account widget (star icon, End of Day row)Generates a suggested offset entry for End of Day amounts at close.
Manual JE in JD EdwardsThe actual closing entry for Carry Forward + Transactions + Exclusions is posted manually in JD Edwards. See the Transfer Order Exclusion Guide for guidance on the exclusion portion.
Offset Account widget

The Offset Account widget is for End of Day only — there is no Transactions offset in this module. After exporting to Excel, replace any "Tolerance Adjust" or "TBD" placeholders with correct GL accounts, then copy the two rightmost columns into JD Edwards. Do not use mid-period — it's a close-only tool.

Confirm Reconciliation

After posting in JD Edwards:

  1. Wait for the next nightly refresh — RapidReconciler does not see your JE until then.
  2. Return to the Reconciliation page.
  3. Confirm Out of Balance = $0.
  4. If a residual variance remains, return to Step 4 and identify which line moved.

About the As-Of Page

The As-Of page reconstructs what was in transit at any prior period end by walking backward from the current Orders page totals.

  • The total will always match the Reconciliation page total for the same period.
  • It will only match the Orders page total if the current period is selected.
  • Calculations are dynamic — backdated activity changes prior As-Of positions.
  • Click the + sign on any row for transaction-level detail in reverse date sequence (sourced from sales/purchase orders, not the item ledger).
  • "Exclusion Adjust" entries in detail are RapidReconciler adjustments, not JD Edwards transactions.

Troubleshooting Common Scenarios

Common issues encountered during reconciliation and how to resolve them.

"The In Transit Validation light is red."

A prior-period issue is affecting carry forward. Hover for details, navigate to the prior period, and resolve the residual variance before continuing.

"GL Batches won't go to zero."

A batch is unposted in JD Edwards. Look for the bell icon flagging batches over 2 days old. If a batch header is missing, run the JDE "Missing Batch Header" report.

"End of Day amount keeps growing."

Sales Update (R42800) is not running for transfer orders. Confirm the version is scheduled, completing successfully, and configured with the A/R interface off.

"I excluded an order, and now its ExclVarQty is non-zero."

A new transaction hit the previously excluded order. Unexclude and re-exclude to recalculate the exclusion amount — do not ignore it.

"The Out of Balance is small but won't clear."

Check the tolerance setting on the Transactions page. Sub-tolerance amounts are excluded from the grid display but still counted in the variance total. Drill into Report 4 — a small UnitPrVar on multiple orders may explain the residual.

"I posted a JE in JDE but RapidReconciler still shows the variance."

Data refreshes nightly. Wait until tomorrow's refresh and re-check.

"The As-Of page total doesn't match the Orders page."

This is expected unless the current period is selected. As-Of reflects historical positions; Orders always reflects the current position.

"I can only see 14 periods of history."

That's the default retention. Contact your administrator to request a purge or extension.

Best Practices and Tips

Habits that make the reconciliation easier, faster, and more reliable over time.

  • Reconcile weekly, not just at month-end. Catching a variance the day after it occurs is dramatically easier than untangling a month of accumulated variances.
  • Always investigate before excluding. An unjustified exclusion hides the real problem and can mask DMAAI configuration issues.
  • Document every transaction note with your name and date. It saves your future self (and the auditor) significant time.
  • Run the Audit Report every period and store it in a dedicated folder. Detail data may be lost in a purge.
  • Treat Integrity Report 4 as an early warning system. Cost variances surface here before they accumulate into period-end pain.
  • Keep the In Transit Sales Update version separate from regular Sales Update. It must have the A/R interface turned off.
  • Coordinate with finance on GL Batches early in the close. Posting batches is often the longest pole in the tent.
  • Use the Drill Down Widget in multi-company environments to identify which entity is driving the variance before diving into transactions.
