Key principle

RapidReconciler does not correct RNV issues — all corrections are made in JD Edwards or through manual journal entries. RapidReconciler's role is to identify which purchase orders are unbalanced, at which line and document, and by how much — so the correct corrective action can be applied efficiently rather than discovered at period end.

Purpose & Scope

The RapidReconciler PO Receipts module reconciles the Received-Not-Vouchered (RNV) balance sheet liability account against open purchase order receipt activity. The RNV account is controlled by DMAAI 4320 and holds the value of goods received from suppliers until the corresponding supplier invoice is matched and vouchered through Accounts Payable.

Receipt table data (F43121) is imported into RapidReconciler and balanced directly against general ledger details (F0902 / F0911), eliminating the need for separate tools or manual matching processes. The module surfaces only the orders and documents that require attention — fully vouchered and reconciled orders are removed automatically.

What is the RNV Account?

The Received-Not-Vouchered (RNV) account is a temporary liability account that holds the value of goods received from a supplier until the corresponding supplier invoice is matched and vouchered. It acts as the bridge between the purchasing and accounts payable processes.

At any point in time, the RNV account balance should equal the value of goods that have been received but not yet matched to a supplier invoice. If the balance does not agree to open receipt activity, a reconciling variance exists.

The Standard Accounting Flow

StepTransactionAccount DebitedAccount CreditedAAI
1PO ReceiptInventoryRNV4310 / 4320
2Voucher MatchRNVA/P Trade4320 / PC

A balanced RNV account therefore shows offsetting debits and credits for every fully processed PO line, with a residual balance that reflects only goods received but not yet vouchered.

Why RNV Reconciliation Is Challenging

Without a dedicated tool, reconciling the RNV account requires manually matching F43121 records to general ledger entries — a time-consuming process that becomes increasingly complex as transaction volume grows. Common challenges include:

ChallengeWhy It's Difficult
VolumeHigh-volume purchasing environments can have thousands of open receipts at any point in time.
Aging receiptsOld receipts that were never vouchered or were incorrectly closed accumulate over time and are difficult to identify manually.
Manual vouchersReceipts cleared through manual journal entries rather than the standard voucher match process leave the F43121 record open, creating a false variance.
Cost variancesPrice variances at voucher match (AAIs 4330, 4332, 4335) can leave residual balances in the RNV account that are difficult to trace.
Currency differencesIn multi-currency environments, exchange rate variances at voucher match (AAI 4340) can create additional reconciling items.
No As-Of reportingThe F43121 table overwrites records on reversal rather than maintaining a transaction history, so reconciling to a prior point in time is not possible from the table itself.

Reconciliation objective

RapidReconciler reconciles the F43121 receipts table to the F0902 GL balance. The goal is not to match every individual voucher to its receipt — that's how JD Edwards processes them. The goal is to identify cases where the two data sources disagree, and to resolve those disagreements.

Key Concepts

Reference material on the data model that powers the PO Receipts module. A working understanding of the F43121 table, Match Types, and the PRLAND field will save significant investigation time when variances appear.

The F43121 Table

The F43121 (PO Receiver) table is the source of truth for RNV reconciliation. It stores a record of every receipt and voucher match transaction on a purchase order line. RapidReconciler imports F43121 data and compares it directly to the corresponding GL entries to identify where the two do not agree.

F43121 Match Types

Each record in F43121 carries a Match Type field that identifies what stage of the purchasing process the record represents. Understanding match types is essential for interpreting the RNV reconciliation.

Match TypeDescriptionEffect on RNV
1Receipt — Created when goods are received against a purchase order (P4312). The initial record that opens the RNV balance.Opens RNV — credits the RNV account via AAI 4320
2Voucher Match — Created when the supplier invoice is matched to the receipt (P4314 / P0411). Written when the Match Type 1 record transitions from open to paid.Closes RNV — debits the RNV account via AAI 4320
3Reversal of Receipt — Created when a receipt is reversed. Offsets the original Match Type 1 record.Closes RNV for the reversed quantity
4Reversal of Voucher Match — Created when a voucher match is reversed.Reopens RNV for the reversed amount
5Landed Cost Receipt — Created when a landed cost is applied at the time of receipt (P4312).Opens RNV for the landed cost amount (AAI 4390)
6Landed Cost Voucher Match — Created when a landed cost is matched to an invoice.Closes RNV for the landed cost amount
Key point

A fully reconciled purchase order line should have offsetting Match Type 1 and Match Type 2 records that net to zero. A line where Match Type 1 exists without a corresponding Match Type 2 represents an open receipt — a legitimate component of the RNV balance. Lines that appear unbalanced for reasons other than a missing voucher match are where reconciling variances originate.

The PRLAND Field

The PRLAND field in F43121 identifies whether a record is a standard receipt or a landed cost, and whether the landed cost is eligible for voucher match:

PRLAND ValueMeaning
BlankStandard product/item receipt line
1Product/item line
2Landed cost — eligible for voucher match
3Landed cost — accrual only, not eligible for voucher match
PRLAND = 3 requires suspension

When PRLAND = 3, the landed cost is accrued at receipt but will never be vouchered. These records contribute to the F43121 open balance but will never have a corresponding Match Type 2 record. They must be suspended in RapidReconciler to avoid creating a permanent false variance.

No Period Selector — Why It Matters

Unlike the Inventory and In Transit modules, the PO Receipts module has no period selector. Because the F43121 table overwrites records on reversal rather than creating new entries, As-Of reporting is not possible. The reconciliation is always performed as a current balance-to-balance comparison — open receipts now vs. GL balance now.

This has two practical implications:

  1. The reconciliation must be performed regularly (weekly is recommended) so that issues are caught while the supporting evidence is still recent.
  2. JD Edwards reports used for comparison should be run at approximately the same time as the most recent RapidReconciler nightly import.
Step 1

Validate the Orders Page

Before comparing RapidReconciler's open receipts total to the GL, validate that the Orders page itself is correct. The reconciliation is only as good as the underlying list — orders that appear here in error will produce phantom variances downstream.

Login & Filters

  1. Log in to RapidReconciler at https://rapidreconciler.getgsi.com
  2. Navigate to the PO Receipts module
  3. Confirm both status lights at the top of the page are green — do not proceed if either is red
  4. Apply the appropriate company and account filters

Validate the Orders List

  1. Set filters to Reconciled = All; Suspended = No
  2. Sort by MostRecReceipt ascending to identify the oldest open receipts
  3. Review every order on the list and decide its disposition:
    Order TypeAction
    Genuinely in transit (recent receipt, voucher pending)Leave open — this is a legitimate component of the RNV balance
    Cleared by manual voucher (P0411 instead of P4314)Correct in JD Edwards if possible; otherwise suspend in RapidReconciler with a note
    PRLAND = 3 landed cost record (accrual only)Suspend — will never be vouchered
    PO at status 999 with no further activity possibleSuspend with a note explaining the closure context
    Aged receipt under active investigationSuspend temporarily with a note describing the investigation
  4. Set filters to Reconciled = All; Suspended = Yes — confirm every suspended order still warrants suspension and has a note explaining why
  5. Confirm the AmtOpen total is accurate before proceeding
Validate before comparing

The open receipts listing must be validated before proceeding with the reconciliation. The quality of the reconciliation depends on the accuracy of the Orders page. Any order on the list in error — either correct in JD Edwards or suspend in RapidReconciler.

Compare to JD Edwards

With filters set to Reconciled = All; Suspended = No, the AmtOpen column total represents the open receipts position. Run an Open Receipts report directly from JD Edwards at approximately the same time as the most recent RapidReconciler nightly import, and compare:

  • If the totals match — proceed to Step 2.
  • If they don't — investigate timing differences first (the two reports may have been run at different points in time). If timing is not the cause, contact your administrator before continuing.

Best practice

Resolve all unreconciled orders before closing the period. Using the Suggested Entry amount to close around outstanding variances defers the problem and accumulates carry-forward balances over time. Reach for Suggested Entry only when an unreconciled order genuinely cannot be addressed in the current cycle.

Step 3

Work Unreconciled Orders

Step 3 is where the actual investigation happens. For each unreconciled order, the goal is to identify the specific document(s) where F43121 disagrees with the GL, determine the root cause, and either correct in JD Edwards or exclude the document with an audit note.

Display Unreconciled Orders

Click the Unreconciled link on the Reconciliation page. This opens the Orders page with filters preset to Reconciled = No; Suspended = No — showing only orders with variances. (Setting these filters manually on the Orders page produces the same result.)

For Each Unreconciled Order

  1. Click the + icon on the left of the order row to expand it
  2. Identify which lines are displayed in grey — these are the unreconciled lines
  3. Click Line Analysis on the grey line. This navigates to the Line Analysis page with filter data pre-populated for that line. (See Line Analysis Page for full reference.)
  4. On the Line Analysis page, change the Rec filter to All and the Line filter to 0, then click Query to display data for the entire order — useful when a variance spans multiple lines
  5. Click the Document button to view the F43121 vs. GL side-by-side comparison
  6. Review the Variance column — identify the specific document(s) showing a non-zero variance
  7. For each variant document, determine whether the discrepancy is in F43121, the GL, or both — see the Variance Patterns reference for common signatures

Take Corrective Action

Each variance has one of two outcomes:

Correctable in JD Edwards
Process the voucher match, reverse the bad transaction, or post a correcting journal entry directly in JDE

After correction, the next nightly refresh will clear the variance from RapidReconciler.

Known exception
Suspend the order or exclude the specific document in RapidReconciler with an audit note

See Suspension vs Exclusion for which to choose.

The Edit Note Action

When a document is being excluded, use the Edit Note button on the Line Analysis page. The Edit Note popup combines two actions in one place:

FieldUse
Excluded checkboxTick to remove the document's amount from the variance calculation after recalculation
Note text boxAudit text — describe why the exclusion is being made (mandatory in practice)

If an exclusion is made in error, unchecking the box reinstates the document. After making any exclusions, click Recalc to refresh the variance calculation. The recalc takes a few minutes — a gear icon in the top right indicates progress.

Helpful trick

On the Line Analysis page, change the line number to 0 and click Query to display data for all lines on the PO. This is extremely useful when diagnosing variances that span multiple lines — the Document button will then show all documents for the entire order in one view.

Step 4

Post the Adjusting JE & Save Audit Documentation

Once all unreconciled orders have been addressed (corrected in JDE or excluded with notes), the period can be closed with a final journal entry and audit documentation produced.

Choose the JE Amount

Two amounts on the Reconciliation page are candidates for the closing journal entry. Which one to use depends on whether unreconciled variances were resolved or carried forward:

If all variances resolved
Use the Out of Balance amount

Posting this entry should drive Out of Balance to $0 after the next nightly refresh.

If carrying variances forward
Use the Suggested Entry amount

This excludes the unreconciled amount, which will reappear next period as Carry Forward.

Common Offset Accounts for RNV Journal Entries

The offset side of the closing JE depends on the nature of the variance being cleared:

SituationOffset Account
Unvouchered receipt to be written offExpense / Cost of Goods Sold
Price variance between receipt and invoicePurchase Price Variance (AAI 4330)
Goods consumed before voucher matchCost of Sales Variance (AAI 4332)
Standard cost varianceAAI 4335
Exchange rate variance (multi-currency)AAI 4340
Correction of a processing errorAppropriate correcting account per the error

Post the JE in JD Edwards

Post the journal entry to the RNV account with the appropriate offset account. RapidReconciler will not see the JE until the next nightly refresh.

Confirm Reconciliation

  1. Wait for the next nightly refresh — RapidReconciler does not see the JE until then
  2. Return to the Reconciliation page
  3. Confirm Out of Balance = $0 (or only the Carry Forward amount, if Suggested Entry was used)
  4. If a residual variance remains, return to Step 3 and identify which line moved

Save Audit Documentation

No dedicated Audit Report button

Unlike the Inventory and In Transit modules, the PO Receipts module does not have a dedicated Audit Report button. Save period-end documentation by:

  1. Exporting the Reconciliation page (screenshot or print to PDF)
  2. Exporting the Orders page to Excel using the green down arrow
  3. Storing both in your audit folder for the period

What Is a Suspension?

A suspension in PO Receipts is equivalent to flagging an order as "Ignore Me." Suspending an order removes its amounts from the out-of-balance and variance calculations.

Common Reasons to Suspend

ReasonDescription
Data cutoff artifactRapidReconciler was loaded by date and only the voucher was imported, not the original receipt. The order processed correctly — it's a start-up issue.
Manual voucher clearanceA supplier invoice was entered using standard voucher entry (P0411) instead of being matched to the PO (P4314), leaving a paid receipt showing as open in F43121. Correcting in JD Edwards is preferred; suspension is the alternative.
PRLAND = 3 landed costAn accrual-only landed cost that will never be vouchered. Suspend to prevent a permanent false variance.
Aged receipt under reviewAn older open receipt being actively investigated. Suspend temporarily with a note explaining the status.
PO at status 999 with no further activity possibleClosed in JD Edwards but still showing residual F43121 balance.
Suspension does not change JD Edwards

Performing a suspension in RapidReconciler does not change any data in JD Edwards. If the order should be corrected in JD Edwards, make that correction first. If an order is suspended in error, it can be unsuspended. Suspension permission must be granted by your administrator.

Best practice

Document the reason for each suspension in the note field for audit purposes. Suspended orders should be reviewed periodically to confirm they remain appropriately suspended and have not had new activity applied against them.

PO Receipts Aging Chart

The aging chart provides a visual breakdown of open receipts by period. Use this to identify whether open receipts are recent (normal) or aging (requires investigation). A large proportion of aged open receipts is a signal that the voucher match process is not keeping pace with receipts, and may indicate:

  • Supplier invoices that were never received or processed
  • Purchase orders that were incorrectly closed without a voucher match
  • Disputed invoices being held outside the normal process
  • System errors that prevented the voucher match from completing

Addressing aging RNV items regularly prevents the balance from accumulating to a point where it becomes difficult to manage.

Reference — Suspension vs. Exclusion

A common point of confusion is when to suspend an order versus when to exclude a document. Both remove amounts from variance calculations, but they operate at different levels.

ActionScopeUse When
Order SuspensionEntire purchase orderThe whole order should be ignored — e.g., PRLAND = 3 landed cost, manual voucher clearance for the entire order, or a data cutoff artifact affecting the whole order
Document ExclusionIndividual document (single receipt or voucher)The order is mostly correct, but a specific document has a known discrepancy — e.g., a single receipt reversed out of sequence, a tax rounding difference, or a manual GL entry tied to one document
Rule of thumb

If the issue affects every line on the order — suspend the order. If the issue is limited to a specific receipt or voucher number — exclude the document.

Reference — Recommended Cadence

RapidReconciler's data refreshes nightly and the F43121 table has no As-Of capability — the reconciliation should be performed on a regular schedule rather than only at period end.

ActivityRecommended Frequency
Review Orders page for aged receiptsWeekly
Audit suspended ordersMonthly
Full reconciliation reviewWeekly
Period-end closing activitiesPeriod-end close

Why Weekly

The F43121 table overwrites records on reversal rather than maintaining a transaction history. This means reconciling to a prior point in time is not possible — once a record changes, the original state is gone. Performing the reconciliation weekly ensures that:

  • Issues are identified while the supporting evidence is still recent
  • Aging receipts don't accumulate to the point of being difficult to investigate
  • Carry-forward balances don't snowball across periods
